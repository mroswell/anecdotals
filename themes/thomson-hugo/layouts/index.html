My mother and aunt were exceptionally gifted in music, and I grew up with a love of music. I learned to play guitar and piano as a child and performed in school musicals. I still enjoy performing on occasion. I also write songs and light verse to entertain my friends, family, and community.

Like my mom, I attended Connecticut College. I studied botany, and became certified to teach high school science. After college, I pursued a technical career doing software development and data visualization. I wrote and published a book on the Drupal web development framework, called Drupal 5 Views.

My current projects combine my interest in technology and my desire to advance climate change mitigation and social justice.

Ever since I can remember, I've been fascinated by the beauty and power of nature. I consider nature as the best guide for figuring out how to live our best lives.

I developed a strong interest in nutrition. I’m certified as a nutrition educator, and I advocate for eating a primarily whole-food, plant-based diet. My perspective is that nutrition — combined with regular exercise, spending time in nature, and participating meaningfully in one’s community — is the best way to live a happy and healthy life.

I have been living in Baltimore since 1984, and consider it my hometown.
