Host a Screening

This is a worksheet
1. Decide to do a screening. Many of us in these times may feel somewhat disconnected. Perhaps we worry that our social network isn't big enough for a screening. But if you feel drawn to this movie, it's worth the effort to strive to share it with others. Be a little bit brave.


2. Choose your screening venue and date. Sample venue options: Your living room; A movie theater; Non-profit meeting room; Zoom (for the whole movie, perhaps, or just for an after-film Q&A after people have watched themselves.) Consider disability access, transportation access., venue cost and size.

3. Consider purchasing a DVD/Blu-Ray as a backup in case the internet streaming is interrupted at your venue.



4. Choose an RSVP tool. Know that if you use Facebook Events, and use phrases like "vaccine injury" your event may be "shadow-banned."
5. Send out invitations.
Copy and Edit (Make it Your Own)
[I’m | Named Organization is] happy to invite you to a screening of Anecdotals on [Date] at [Location name and address]. The movie is a compassionate exploration of the nuanced vaccine debate. [Mention your connection to the film]. [if panel discussion: The film will be followed by a panel discussion including…] [if organic food is served: Light organic fare will be served] [Details about parking]
6. Consider a panel discussion with vaccine-injured participants, advocates, and medical professionals
7. Enjoy your role as host! Thank your participants!
8. Share on social media using
